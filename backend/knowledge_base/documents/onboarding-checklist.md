# New Employee IT Onboarding Checklist

## Overview

Welcome to the company! This checklist ensures you have all the IT resources needed to be productive from day one. Your manager and IT will coordinate most of these items before your start date, but some steps require your action.

## Before Your First Day (Handled by IT)

1. **Account creation** — Active Directory, email (user@company.com), and SSO (Okta) accounts are provisioned based on the hiring ticket from HR.
2. **Hardware assignment** — A laptop, docking station, monitor, keyboard, and mouse are prepared and asset-tagged. Equipment ships to remote employees via tracked courier.
3. **Access groups** — You are added to department-specific security groups, distribution lists, and shared drives based on your role.
4. **Software pre-installation** — Standard applications (Office 365, Slack, Zoom, Cisco AnyConnect) are deployed to your device.

## Your First Day

### Step 1: Activate Your Account
1. Power on your laptop and log in with the temporary credentials provided in your welcome email (sent to your personal email).
2. You will be prompted to change your password. Choose a strong password (12+ characters, mixed case, numbers, special character).
3. Set up Multi-Factor Authentication immediately — follow the MFA guide in the security-policies document.

### Step 2: Configure Your Email
1. Open Microsoft Outlook — your corporate mailbox is already linked.
2. Set up email on your mobile device following the email-setup guide.
3. Verify calendar sync is working by checking for your onboarding meeting invites.

### Step 3: Install Additional Software
1. Open the **Company Portal** app and browse the catalog.
2. Install any role-specific tools recommended by your manager (IDE, design tools, etc.).
3. Submit requests for software not in the catalog via ServiceNow.

### Step 4: Connect to the Network
1. In-office: Connect to **Corp-Secure** WiFi using your corporate credentials.
2. Remote: Install and connect to the VPN using Cisco AnyConnect (see vpn-setup guide).

### Step 5: Badge Activation
1. Visit the Security desk in the lobby (Building A, ground floor) with a photo ID.
2. Your employee badge is programmed for building access, printer authentication, and cafeteria payments.
3. Remote employees receive a badge by mail; it is pre-activated and ready to use when visiting an office.

## First Week Tasks

- [ ] Join your department's Slack/Teams channels (list provided by your manager).
- [ ] Verify access to shared drives and SharePoint sites relevant to your role.
- [ ] Complete the mandatory **Security Awareness Training** in the learning portal within 7 days.
- [ ] Test printing by sending a test page to your floor's printer and releasing with your badge.
- [ ] Bookmark key internal sites: Intranet, ServiceNow, HR portal, knowledge base.
